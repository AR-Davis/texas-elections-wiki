Frequently Asked Questions

When are the next Texas elections?

The next general election is November 3, 2026 (federal/state/county offices). Primary elections will be held March 3, 2026 with runoffs May 26, 2026. Uniform elections for political subdivisions occur on May 2, 2026.

How do I register to vote in Texas?

Texas requires voters to register at least 30 days before an election. You can register by mail using Form H-13 (available at sos.state.tx.us) or in person at your county voter registrar's office. Texas does not currently offer online voter registration.

What ID do I need to vote?

Texas requires photo ID: Texas driver's license, Texas Election Identification Certificate, Texas personal ID card, handgun license, military ID, citizenship certificate, or passport. Voters without acceptable ID may vote provisionally if they fill out a Reasonable Impediment Declaration.

How do I vote by mail?

Texas restricts mail ballots to: 65+, disabled, out of county during election, incarcerated but eligible, or expected childbirth. Submit Application for Ballot by Mail (ABBM) to your county clerk starting January 1 of election year. Deadline is Feb 21 for March 3, 2026 primary.

Who is my state representative?

Find your representatives at capitol.texas.gov using the "Who Represents Me?" tool. The Texas Legislature has 150 House districts and 31 Senate districts.
