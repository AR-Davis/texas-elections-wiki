Travis County Voting Location Equity Analysis

Geographic and demographic disparities in extended hours voting access

Executive Summary: Analysis reveals a geographic disparity in Travis County's extended hours voting locations, with wealthy, low-density areas receiving disproportionate access compared to dense, lower-income neighborhoods.

Key Finding: Inverse Relationship

Core Disparity: Wealthy, low-density areas have extended hours; dense, lower-income areas do not.

Poorer Eastern Areas

Critical Finding: Lakeway Activity Center serves 934 people/sq mi in a community averaging $715,000 income while dense, lower-income East Austin (5-15x the population density) lacks equivalent extended hours access.

Data Methodology

  • Voting Locations: Travis County Clerk — 2024 Early Voting Locations (43 total sites, 7 extended hours)
  • Demographics: Zip Atlas — Austin Metro ZIP code population density and income data (IRS AGI, 2021)
  • Geographic Classification: West vs. East Austin ZIP code classification based on I-35 divide

Policy Implications

  • Carver Library (78702) is the only extended hours site in Eastern Travis County
  • 78705 (West Campus/UT) has highest density (15,752/sq mi) but no dedicated extended hours site
  • Consider transit access times to extended hours locations for equity assessment
  • Analysis suggests need for equity-focused voting access policy review
[1] Travis County Clerk, 2024 Early Voting Locations; [2] Zip Atlas (IRS AGI 2021)
